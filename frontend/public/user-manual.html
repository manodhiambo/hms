1 System Overview & Roles

The Hospital Management System (HMS) is a web-based platform that streamlines all clinical and administrative operations — from patient registration and clinical consultations to billing, laboratory, pharmacy, and management reports.

User Roles

Access to modules is controlled by user roles. Each staff member is assigned exactly one role:

RoleBadgePrimary Access
Super AdminSUPER_ADMINFull access to all modules including data management and audit logs
Hospital AdminHOSPITAL_ADMINAll modules except system-level data tools
DoctorDOCTORConsultations, prescriptions, lab & imaging orders
NurseNURSETriage, nursing notes, wards, patient check-in
PharmacistPHARMACISTPharmacy inventory, dispensing, prescriptions
Lab TechnicianLAB_TECHLab orders queue, processing results
RadiologistRADIOLOGISTImaging orders queue, completing scans
ReceptionistRECEPTIONISTPatients, appointments, visits check-in
AccountantACCOUNTANTBilling, payments, insurance claims, reports

2 Logging In & Navigation

Logging In

  1. Open the HMS in your web browser. You will see the login screen.
  2. Enter your Email address and Password provided by your administrator.
  3. Click Sign In. You will be taken to the Dashboard.
Note: If you forget your password, contact your System Administrator to reset it. Do not share your credentials with colleagues.

Navigation Sidebar

The left sidebar contains links to all modules. The modules visible to you depend on your role. Common items include:

  • Dashboard — Live KPIs and alerts
  • Patients — Registration and records
  • Appointments — Schedule management
  • Triage — Patient priority queue
  • Consultations — Clinical visits
  • Pharmacy — Drug inventory & dispensing
  • Laboratory — Test orders & results
  • Imaging — Radiology orders
  • Billing — Invoices & payments
  • Wards — Admissions & nursing
  • Reports — Financial & clinical reports
  • Settings — Hospital profile (Admin only)
Tip: Click the bell icon in the top bar to see unread notifications. Your profile icon shows your name and role.

4 Patient Management

Navigate to Patients in the sidebar. All registered patients are listed here with their name, patient number, phone, gender, and date of birth.

4a. Register a Patient

  1. Click the New Patient button (top-right).
  2. Fill in the required fields: Full Name, Date of Birth, Gender, Phone Number.
  3. Enter optional fields as available: ID/Passport Number, Address, Email, Next of Kin, Blood Group, Allergies.
  4. Click Save Patient. The system auto-generates a unique Patient Number.
Duplicate Check: The system automatically checks if a patient with the same phone number or ID already exists and warns you before saving.

4b. Search for a Patient

Use the Search bar at the top of the Patients page. You can search by:

  • Full name (partial match supported)
  • Patient number (e.g. PT-00123)
  • Phone number
  • ID / Passport number

Results appear instantly as you type (after 2 characters). Click any row to open the patient's full record.

4c. Edit Patient Record

  1. Click on a patient row to open their detail page.
  2. Click Edit.
  3. Update the required fields and click Save.
Tip: The patient record shows visit history at the bottom. Click any visit to navigate directly to that consultation.

5 Appointments

Manage scheduled appointments via the Appointments module.

Book an Appointment

  1. Click New Appointment.
  2. Search for and select the patient.
  3. Choose the Doctor and appointment Date & Time.
  4. Add an optional reason/notes, then click Save.

Appointment Statuses

StatusMeaning
SCHEDULEDAppointment booked, patient not yet arrived
CONFIRMEDPatient confirmed via phone/in-person
COMPLETEDPatient attended and visit was created
CANCELLEDAppointment cancelled
NO_SHOWPatient did not arrive

Click the status badge on any appointment to update it.

6 Triage Queue

Triage is performed by NURSE staff when a patient arrives. Navigate to Triage in the sidebar.

Performing Triage

  1. The queue shows all patients who have a visit created but not yet triaged.
  2. Click Triage on the relevant patient row.
  3. Assign a Priority:
    • IMMEDIATE — Life-threatening, needs immediate attention
    • URGENT — Serious condition, see within 30 minutes
    • LESS_URGENT — Stable, can wait up to 2 hours
    • NON_URGENT — Routine visit
  4. Record initial vitals (BP, Temp, Pulse, etc.) and any triage notes.
  5. Click Save Triage. The visit moves to the doctor's queue.
Emergency Protocol: IMMEDIATE priority patients should be taken directly to the doctor and triaged retrospectively if needed.

7 Consultations (Visits)

The Consultations module manages all clinical visits. Doctors see their queue here; receptionists can create new visits.

7a. Create a New Visit

  1. Click New Visit.
  2. Search for the patient by name or number. Select them from the dropdown.
  3. Optionally assign a Doctor.
  4. Select Visit Type: OPD (outpatient), IPD (inpatient), or Emergency.
  5. Enter the Chief Complaint (why the patient is presenting).
  6. Click Create Visit.
Search: Use the search bar on the Consultations list to quickly find a visit by patient name or number.

7b. Clinical Notes & Vitals

  1. Click any visit row to open the Visit Detail page.
  2. Click Edit to begin entering clinical information.
  3. Fill in the clinical sections:
    • Chief Complaint — Patient's primary symptom
    • Presenting Illness — Detailed history
    • Examination — Physical findings
    • Diagnosis — Start typing a disease name or ICD-11 code; an autocomplete dropdown will appear. Select the correct diagnosis to auto-fill the ICD-11 code.
    • Treatment Plan — Planned management
    • Doctor Notes — Any additional notes
  4. Enter Vital Signs on the right panel: Blood Pressure, Temperature, Pulse, Respiratory Rate, Weight, Height, O₂ Saturation.
  5. Click Save.

7c. Prescriptions

  1. In the Visit Detail page, click + Prescribe in the Prescriptions card.
  2. In the modal, type the drug name in the search box. Results come from the pharmacy inventory.
  3. Select the drug from the dropdown. Its stock quantity is shown.
  4. Enter Dosage (e.g. 500mg), Frequency (e.g. 3× daily), Duration (e.g. 5 days), and Quantity.
  5. Add any special Instructions (e.g. take after meals).
  6. Click Create Prescription.
Dispensing: Once prescribed, the pharmacist will see the prescription in their Pending Prescriptions queue and dispense it.

7d. Lab Orders

  1. Click + Order Lab in the Lab Orders card.
  2. Select the required test from the dropdown (shows test name, code, and price).
  3. Click Create Lab Order.

The lab technician will process the order. When results are ready, a banner appears on the visit page for the doctor to review.

7e. Imaging Orders

In the Imaging Orders card, additional imaging (X-ray, MRI, CT Scan, etc.) can be requested. The radiologist will process and enter findings.

7f. Completing a Visit

  1. Before completing, click Send to Billing to automatically create a billing invoice populated with all prescriptions, lab tests, and imaging ordered during the visit.
  2. Once the consultation is finished, click Complete Visit. This locks the visit from further editing.

Print & Share

  • Print Report — Opens a formatted diagnosis report suitable for printing.
  • WhatsApp — Sends the diagnosis report directly to the patient's WhatsApp number.

8 Pharmacy

8a. Drug Inventory

The Drug Inventory tab lists all drugs currently stocked. Each drug record includes:

  • Generic name, brand name, category, formulation, strength
  • Quantity in stock, reorder level
  • Batch number, expiry date, supplier
  • Cost price, selling price

Add a New Drug

  1. Click Add Drug.
  2. Fill in all drug details and click Save.

Edit / Restock a Drug

  1. Click the pencil icon on any drug row.
  2. Update the quantity or any other field and click Update.
Low Stock Alert: Drugs at or below their reorder level are highlighted and appear in the Dashboard alerts. Restock them promptly.

8b. Dispensing Prescriptions

  1. Go to the Pending Prescriptions tab.
  2. Review the prescription: patient name, drug, dosage, quantity.
  3. Click Dispense to mark the prescription as dispensed and deduct from inventory.

8c. Refunds

If a patient returns dispensed medication, a refund can be initiated from the Refunds module. An admin will review and approve or reject the refund request.

9 Laboratory

Navigate to Laboratory. It has two tabs: Orders Queue and Test Catalog.

Orders Queue

Lab orders flow through the following statuses:

StatusAction RequiredDone By
ORDEREDCollect patient sampleLab Technician
SAMPLE_COLLECTEDProcess the sample and enter resultLab Technician
COMPLETEDVerify the resultLab Technician / Senior
VERIFIEDRelease result to doctorLab Technician
RELEASEDDoctor reviews resultDoctor

Click the status filter buttons to see orders at each stage. Use the patient search box to filter by patient name or number within the current status.

Creating a New Lab Order (from Lab page)

  1. Click New Order.
  2. Search for the patient by name or number, then select them.
  3. Select the patient's relevant visit from the dropdown.
  4. Choose the test to be performed.
  5. Click Create Order.

Entering a Test Result

  1. When an order is in SAMPLE_COLLECTED status, click Process.
  2. Enter the Result value, check Mark as Abnormal if applicable, and add any Remarks.
  3. Click Submit Result. The order moves to COMPLETED status.

Test Catalog

Manage the list of available lab tests. Each test has a name, code, category, sample type, reference range, unit, and price. Admins and lab technicians can add or edit tests.

10 Imaging (Radiology)

The Imaging module manages X-rays, MRI, CT scans, ultrasounds, and other imaging orders.

Workflow

  1. A doctor orders imaging from within a Visit (Imaging Orders card).
  2. The radiologist sees the pending order in the Imaging module.
  3. After completing the scan, the radiologist clicks Complete, enters Findings and Impression.
  4. The findings appear on the visit page and are included in the printed diagnosis report.

Filter orders by status: PENDING, IN_PROGRESS, COMPLETED.

11 Billing & Payments

11a. Create an Invoice

  1. Go to Billing.
  2. Click New Invoice.
  3. Search for the patient and select them.
  4. Optionally link a Visit ID (or leave blank for a standalone invoice).
  5. Click Create Invoice. The invoice opens automatically.
Auto-populate: When "Send to Billing" is clicked from a Visit, an invoice is created and automatically populated with all prescriptions, lab tests, and imaging charges from that visit.

11b. Add Services to an Invoice

  1. Open an invoice and click + Add Service.
  2. Optionally pick from the Services Catalog (quick-fill buttons).
  3. Select Service Type. If you select Pharmacy, a drug search box appears — type the drug name to search the pharmacy inventory and auto-fill the description and price.
  4. Enter or confirm: Description, Quantity, Unit Price.
  5. Click Add Service.

11c. Record a Payment

  1. Open the invoice and click Record Payment.
  2. Choose the payment method: Cash, M-Pesa, Card, Bank Transfer, Insurance, or Donation.
  3. Enter the amount and reference number (for M-Pesa, card, or bank).
  4. For split payments (multiple methods), click Split Payment and add each payment entry.
  5. Click Record Payment. The invoice status updates automatically: PENDING → PARTIAL → PAID.

11d. Insurance

For insurance-covered patients:

  1. Open the invoice and click the edit (pencil) icon.
  2. Enter the Insurance Covered Amount. This reduces the patient's balance.
  3. Record any remaining co-payment as a normal payment.

Invoice Statuses

StatusMeaning
PENDINGNo payment received yet
PARTIALPartial payment received
PAIDFully paid
WAIVEDBill waived by administration
REFUNDEDPayment refunded
Printing Receipts: Use the Print Invoice button on an open invoice to generate a printable receipt/invoice PDF.

12 Wards & Admissions

Manage inpatient admissions in the Wards module.

Admitting a Patient

  1. Go to Wards → Admissions.
  2. Click Admit Patient.
  3. Search for the patient and select them.
  4. Choose the Ward, Room, and Bed from the available options.
  5. Enter the Diagnosis and Admission Notes.
  6. Click Admit.

Nursing Notes

Nurses can add ongoing nursing notes to each admission. Click on an active admission and use the Add Nursing Note button.

Discharging a Patient

  1. Open the patient's admission.
  2. Click Discharge.
  3. Enter a Discharge Summary.
  4. Click Confirm Discharge. The bed is freed automatically.

13 Insurance Claims

Track and process insurance claims via Billing → Insurance Claims.

  1. Click New Claim.
  2. Select the Insurance Company, Patient, and Invoice.
  3. Enter the Claimed Amount and any reference or policy number.
  4. Click Submit Claim.

15 User Management

Available to SUPER_ADMIN and HOSPITAL_ADMIN only.

Create a New User

  1. Go to Users in the sidebar.
  2. Click New User.
  3. Fill in: Full Name, Email, Role, Department (optional), Specialization (for doctors), Password.
  4. Click Create User. The user can now log in with their email and password.

Edit a User

Click the pencil icon on any user row to update their profile. Click the lock icon to reset their password.

Deactivate / Delete a User

Use the delete icon. Only Super Admins can permanently delete users. Deleted users' records are preserved for audit purposes.

16 Settings

Available to SUPER_ADMIN and HOSPITAL_ADMIN only.

Navigate to Settings to update the hospital's branding and contact information:

  • Hospital Name — Appears on all printed documents
  • Tagline — Sub-title shown on reports and receipts
  • Address — Physical address
  • Phone — Contact number
  • Email — Hospital email address

Click Save Settings to apply changes. These details appear on all printed invoices, receipts, and diagnosis reports.

Medical Services Catalog

The Services Catalog (accessible from the Billing module settings) contains pre-defined service items (consultation fees, procedure charges, etc.) that can be quickly added to invoices. Admins can add, edit, and deactivate services.

17 Notifications

The bell icon in the top navigation bar shows unread notifications. Notifications are generated automatically for:

  • New lab results released to a doctor
  • Prescriptions dispensed or rejected
  • Low stock drug alerts
  • New admissions or discharges

Click a notification to navigate to the relevant record. Click Mark all as read to clear the badge.

18 Troubleshooting & Support

Common Issues

ProblemSolution
Cannot log inCheck your email and password. Contact your admin to reset your password.
Page shows no dataCheck your internet connection. Refresh the page (F5). If it persists, contact support.
Cannot complete a visitEnsure you have saved all clinical notes. Completed visits cannot be edited.
Drug not found in searchThe drug may not be in the pharmacy inventory. Ask the pharmacist to add it first.
Invoice balance incorrectCheck if insurance covered amount is set on the invoice. Re-verify item quantities and prices.
Lab result not showingThe result must be RELEASED by the lab technician before it appears on the visit.
Bed not availableCheck the Wards module for currently occupied vs. available beds. Discharge a patient first if needed.
